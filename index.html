Rabaul History

Rabaul is a township in East New Britain province, on the island of New Britain, in the country of Papua New Guinea. New Britain is an island about 60 kilometers to the east of the island of New Guinea. East New Britain is a province of Papua New Guinea, consisting of the north-eastern part of the island of New Britain and the Duke of York Islands. The Capital of the province is Kokopo, 40km from the old capital of Rabaul which was know as the first city of New Guinea during the colonial era and was largely destroyed during the twin volcanic eruption in 1994. 

East New Britain covers a total land area of 15,816 square kilometers (6,107 sq mi), and the province's population was reported as 220,133 in the 2000 census, rising to 500,000 Est in the 2017 count. Provincial coastal waters extend over an area of 104,000 square meters (26 acres). The province's only land border is with West New Britain Province to the west, and it also shares a maritime border with New Ireland Province to the east.  Rabaul is better known as (Rababaul) in our Kuanua language meaning "The Mangroves". We come from a Melanesian Matrilineal Society where the woman is rulers and own the Land and Clans. We speak the Kuanua Language and we are known as the Kuanua people of Rabaul and NOT the Tolais. Tolai in Kuanua meaning "My Friend" is just a slang used in the early 1960s.   

The people of East New Britain are culturally diversified, with rich and unique traditions. The "tumbuan", featured in many cultural dress costumes, signifies spiritual dancers and traditional ceremonies. The Kuanua people of the Gazelle Peninsula have continued to use the traditional shell money called "tabu". East New Britain has a bit of everything; colonial and war exciting wilderness and a great local culture, Rabaul sits at the base of an extinct volcanic caldron which has to be most famous for its active volcanoes and its world class diving geographical sites.

Brief histories of Sailors and Missionaries Journey within New Guinea Islands and the Bismarck Archipelago. 


* 1500 16th Century Sailors from Europe sailed through the Pacific Ocean as Traders. 

* 1528 Bismarck Archipelago a Spain Sailor De Saavedra drew the islands of Manus.

* 1768 a group of sailors from France, who followed England, sailors saw Bougainville.

* 1791 English sailor from England named John Hunter sailed from Sydney on his way to Batavia (Indonesia) stopped over at Port Hunter          named after him and he made friends with the people at Molot Duke of York Islands and gave them two nice puppies he brought all the          way from England.

* 1793 d'Entrecasteaux & Duperrey in 1823 from England named Simpson first discovered the Simpson Harbor in his Ship called Blanche          now the other bay called Blanche Bay.

* 1848 Roman Catholic Church first arrived in Woodlark Islands & Umboi.

* 1875 Germany sent Von Schleinitz in a Ship called "Gazelle" and now we called it "Gazelle Peninsula".

* 15th August 1875 Methodist Church first Missionaries led by Dr. George Brown & 10 Fijians first arrived at Port Hunter, Molot, Duke of York    Island.

* 1889 Roman Catholic First arrived in Matupit Island & Nodup village in Rabaul and later moved to Vunapope now it is today.

* 4th June 1929 Captain G F Jones with Fijian missionaries of the Seventh-Day Adventist Church first set foot at Patami beach (Raulai) Matupit   Island Rabaul.
